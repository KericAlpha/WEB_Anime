The story of anime:

In January the 1. 1963, the first anime, which had around 24 minutes and had a continued action, called "Astro Boy" from Osamu Tezuka was released on Fuji TV. Osamu Tezuka found a company called Tezuka Productions, which he later called Mushi Productions. In october the 6. 1965, Osamu Tezuka and his company released the first coloured anime called "Kimba, the white lion". More and more mangas got an anime adaption and so the japanese cartoons became more famous internationally and were broadcast on american and even german tv. The 1980s were the golden age for anime, because more companys were found, more animes were made and more animes got broadcast worldwide. The word "Otaku" was born during the golden age. Otakus are anime fans who spend a lot of money on anime products. The most well known animes, that got released around the 1990s, were "Sailor Moon", "Pokemon", "Yu-Gi-Oh" and "Digimon".

Anime nowadays:

Nowadays, animes are well known in all countries for their great animation and story telling. The big three called "Dragonball", "Naruto" and "One Piece" are the most famous animes in the world. The Ghibli Studios, an anime studio, is well known for his anime movies like "My Neighbour Totoro", "Princess Mononoke", "Spirited Away" and "Howl's Moving Castle". The fanbase also got really big in the past few years and it's getting bigger nowadays. Animes would not excist without anime studios. The most known studios are A-1 Pictures, Bee Train, Bones, Madhouse, Studio Pierrot, Sunrise and Toei Animation.

What does "Anime" mean?

In Japanese "anime" can refer to any animation film, both from their own country and from abroad. Outside of Japan, we use it for animated films from Japan. Before it got any famous, it was called senga ("line art") and kuga ("folding pictures"), dekobo shin gacho ("mischievous new images") or chamebo-zu ("playful pictures"). Later on the words manga eiga ("manga-movies") and doga ("movable pictures") got used. In the 1970s the word anime appears in Japan together with the word creation Japanimation. Anime originated as a shortening for the Japanese lain word animeshon ("animation"). At the end of the 1970s in the english speaking area, the word japanimation got used, and later on, in the 1990s, the word anime got used, because japanimation lead to misunderstandings.
